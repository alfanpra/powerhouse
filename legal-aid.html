What is Legal Aid?

Public legal funding for private defence.

Legal Aid NSW is a government organisation that provides legal services to socially and economically disadvantaged people across NSW. They fund private criminal lawyers — like us — to act for clients who could not otherwise afford serious criminal defence.

Principal Solicitor Chadi Irani is on both the Legal Aid NSW Serious Crime Panel and the General Crime Panel — appointments held by a small minority of NSW defence lawyers. When Legal Aid approves a grant of aid, our firm can appear in court and run your defence at no out-of-pocket cost to you.

We accept Legal Aid briefs from 10 Sydney-area Legal Aid offices and defend clients in matters including sexual assault, robbery, drug offences and other serious indictable charges across every NSW court.

The decision on whether you qualify is made by Legal Aid NSW, not by us — but we will help you complete the application, gather the supporting documents, and lodge it. If aid is granted, your matter proceeds with our firm as your nominated solicitor.

How to Apply

The path to a Grant of Aid.

A clear, four-step process. We do the heavy lifting on the paperwork so you can focus on your matter and your court date.

Step 01

Contact us

Free first consultation. We listen to your matter, your court date and your circumstances — no obligation, no jargon.

Step 02

Eligibility check

We assess your situation against Legal Aid NSW's tests — the means test, the merits test, and jurisdictional rules for your charge.

Step 03

Lodge a Grant of Aid

We complete the application form, help you arrange supporting documents, and lodge it on your behalf with Legal Aid NSW.

Step 04

Your defence begins

Once aid is granted, we appear as your nominated solicitor — through bail, mentions, hearings and any appeal that follows.

Not Eligible?

Don't worry — we still want to help.

If Legal Aid NSW determines you do not qualify for a grant, you have not run out of options. Powerhouse Law offers transparent fixed quotes and structured payment plans for every matter we take on.

Our lawyers accept payment plans and allow you to pay legal fees in instalments — so you can access professional criminal defence without worrying about the cost up front.

  • Transparent fixed quotes — no hourly surprises.
  • Instalment plans tailored to your circumstances.
  • Free first consultation — quote provided up front, in writing.
  • No charge for the eligibility check itself.
