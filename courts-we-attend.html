The NSW Court Hierarchy

Which court hears which matter.

New South Wales runs a three-tier criminal court system, with the Children's Court sitting alongside the Local Court for matters involving young people. Where your matter is heard shapes how it should be defended.

The Local Court is the entry point for almost every criminal and traffic matter in NSW. It hears summary offences in full and conducts committal proceedings for indictable matters before they go up.

The District Court is the intermediate court — it hears strictly indictable charges (with two exceptions, murder and treason), runs jury trials, and sits on appeal from the Local Court for severity and conviction appeals.

The Supreme Court of NSW hears the most serious matters — murder, treason, and any case where the law itself is unsettled. Sitting alongside it, the Court of Criminal Appeal is the appellate court for all serious criminal matters from the District and Supreme Courts.

The Children's Court is a specialist jurisdiction for accused persons under 18 at the time of the alleged offence — running with its own rules, its own atmosphere, and its own approach to outcomes.

The Courts

Where we appear.

Four court tiers. One defence team. We appear daily across every court in the NSW criminal justice system, from urgent first mentions to multi-week jury trials.

Tier 1 · Summary

The Local Court.

The entry point for almost every criminal and traffic matter in NSW. The Local Court hears all summary offences, conducts committals for indictable matters, and deals with the bulk of the day-to-day criminal calendar.

  • All criminal & traffic charges
  • Bail applications
  • Defended hearings
  • Sentence hearings
  • Licence appeals
  • Committals
  • AVOs
  • Breach of bond
  • Agent appearances
Specialist Jurisdiction

The Children's Court.

A specialist jurisdiction for accused persons under 18 at the time of the alleged offence, including traffic offences for young people not yet eligible for a driver's licence. The procedures, sentencing principles and atmosphere are fundamentally different from the adult courts.

  • Bail applications
  • Defended hearings
  • Sentence hearings
  • AVOs
  • Serious indictable offences
  • Committals
Tier 2 · Indictable

The District Court.

The intermediate court of NSW — handling strictly indictable charges other than murder and treason. Jury trials, complex sentencing, and the appellate jurisdiction for matters coming up from the Local Court.

  • Armed robbery
  • Wounding / GBH
  • Dangerous driving causing death
  • Drug supply / manufacture
  • Sexual assault
  • Firearms offences
  • Jury trials
  • Severity & conviction appeals
  • Section 32 appeals
Tier 3 · Most Serious

Supreme Court & CCA.

The Supreme Court of NSW hears the most serious criminal matters — including murder and treason. Alongside it, the Court of Criminal Appeal is the appellate court for serious criminal matters arising from the District and Supreme Courts.

  • Murder & manslaughter
  • Treason
  • Supreme Court bail
  • Appellate jurisdiction
  • Crown appeals
  • Cases stated
  • Conviction appeals
