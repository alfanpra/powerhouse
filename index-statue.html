Frequently Asked

Common questions about our process, fees, and how we can help in your case.

Should I do a Police interview or remain silent?

You have a fundamental right to remain silent. You can freely refuse to answer Police questions and do not have to make a statement. Always seek legal advice before deciding.

Police cannot say you are guilty because you refused to answer questions. Remaining silent is your right and often the best strategic choice.

Can you get my charge dropped before going to court?

In many cases, yes. We can write to Police requesting charges be withdrawn where evidence is weak or procedural issues exist. Early intervention is often the most effective strategy.

What is a Section 10 and how do I get one?

A Section 10 means you are found guilty but no conviction is recorded — your criminal record stays clear. We regularly achieve Section 10 outcomes for first offences, traffic, drug possession and assault matters.

Do you accept Legal Aid and offer payment plans?

Yes. We accept Legal Aid assignments and can assist with applications. We also offer transparent fixed fees and flexible payment plans for privately funded matters.

How quickly can you help if I've just been arrested?

Immediately. Our team is available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year. Call 1800 100 529 and a senior lawyer will assist you. For urgent bail we appear the same day, including weekends and public holidays.
