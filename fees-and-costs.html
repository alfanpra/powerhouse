Three Billing Pathways

Pick the structure that fits your matter.

Different matters call for different fee structures. We will walk you through each during the first consultation and recommend the one that best aligns with your case and your circumstances.

1. Fixed-fee retainer

The default for the majority of our matters. A single agreed amount covers a defined scope — for example, the entire Local Court process from first mention to sentencing, or a Licence Appeal from filing to determination. If the matter takes longer than we expected, the fee does not change.

2. Staged fee

For longer matters with uncertain trajectory — committals, trials, complex negotiations — we break the work into discrete stages, each with its own fixed fee. You pay for the stage before it begins and decide at each checkpoint whether to continue. No commitment to fund a trial when you are still running mentions.

3. Legal Aid grant

If you are eligible, the Legal Aid NSW grant covers your representation in full. Eligibility turns on the means test, the merits test, and whether the charge is within scope. The grant decision is made by Legal Aid, not by us — we help you prepare and lodge the application, and the eligibility check itself is free. See our Legal Aid NSW page for full detail.

Inclusions & Exclusions

Exactly what's in the quote.

Transparency does not stop at the total. Every quote breaks down what is included and what is not, so there is no debate about scope later. The two columns below are representative — your written quote will set out your matter specifically.

Always Included
Your fixed fee covers
  • Free first consultation and case assessment
  • Written advice on prospects and strategy
  • All correspondence with the prosecution and the court
  • Review of the police brief of evidence
  • Negotiation of facts and charge particulars
  • Drafting of written submissions on sentence
  • Coordination of character references and subjective material
  • Local Court appearances within the agreed scope
  • Phone and email access throughout the matter
Quoted Separately
Costs that depend on the matter
  • Barrister's brief on District / Supreme Court trial
  • Expert reports (psychiatrist, accident reconstruction, toxicology)
  • Subpoena issuing fees and witness conduct money
  • Court filing fees and transcript costs
  • Interstate or remote-court travel and accommodation
  • Translation and interpreter services
  • Matters that escalate beyond the original scope

Anything in the right-hand column is quoted separately and in writing before we incur it. We do not commit you to a disbursement without telling you the cost first. If a matter genuinely escalates beyond the original scope — for example, a Local Court matter that gets committed up — we re-quote at that point, with the same fixed-fee approach.
