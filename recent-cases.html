The Cases

Six matters. Six wins.

A representative cross-section of recent Powerhouse Law results — from indictable criminal matters in the District and Supreme Courts to local-court traffic appeals. Different facts, different charges, one constant.

Sexual Assault · s.61I District Court · Sydney
Penalty Faced 14 years max + Sex Offender Register · Career end
Outcome Acquitted · Not guilty Jury verdict · No record

M.K., a young professional with no prior history, was charged with sexual assault following an alleged incident at a private function. The Crown case rested on credibility. We cross-examined to expose timeline inconsistencies, deployed contemporaneous messages, and ran the affirmative-consent defence. Jury returned a unanimous not-guilty verdict after four hours.

"We honestly never thought we'd get through this. Powerhouse fought for us every single day until the jury came back."

— M.K., Acquitted Client
Supply Prohibited Drug · Indictable District Court · Parramatta
Penalty Faced 15 years max Commercial supply · Standard NPP
Outcome Charges withdrawn Personal-use plea on lesser matter

A.R. faced commercial-supply charges following a residential search. We brought a successful application under s.138 Evidence Act excluding the principal evidence on illegal-search grounds. The Crown reviewed the brief and withdrew the supply charge entirely — the residual matter resolved on a non-custodial outcome.

"I was facing fifteen years. Chadi found the evidence problem the Crown didn't see. The charges were gone."

— A.R., Charges Withdrawn
Mid Range PCA · Drink Driving Local Court · Penrith
Penalty Faced 6-month suspension + $2,200 fine · Conviction recorded
Outcome Section 10 dismissal No conviction · Career preserved

J.T., a registered nurse, faced loss of her professional registration on a recorded conviction. We obtained character evidence from the hospital, an in-depth treatment plan, and submissions establishing the offence as entirely out of character. The Magistrate granted a Section 10 dismissal — no conviction recorded.

"I would have lost my career. The Section 10 saved my registration, my licence, and my work."

— J.T., Registered Nurse
Bail Application · Indictable Supreme Court of NSW
Position Faced Refused twice Local Court + District Court
Outcome Bail granted Released same day · Conditions met

After two refusals on serious indictable charges, we ran a Supreme Court bail application built around changed circumstances — new approved accommodation, a written employment offer, an acceptable surety. The Justice granted bail with conditions and our client was released the same day.

"Two courts said no. Powerhouse got me out the same day at the Supreme Court. I'll never forget what that meant for my family."

— S.B., Bail Granted
Assault Occasioning ABH · s.59 Local Court · Bankstown
Penalty Faced 5 years max + Conviction · ICO likely
Outcome Section 10 dismissal No conviction · No record

D.S., a young professional with no prior history, was charged after a late-night altercation outside a Sydney venue. We obtained a psychological report, comprehensive character references, and a structured treatment plan. The Magistrate accepted the matter was aberrant conduct and granted a Section 10 dismissal.

"It was one night. They saved me from carrying it for the rest of my career."

— D.S., No Conviction Recorded
Speed over 30km/h Local Court · Licence Appeal
Penalty Faced 3-month suspension Automatic · Loss of livelihood
Outcome Suspension overturned Licence retained · Demerits preserved

P.B., a Sydney courier and sole income earner for his family, faced losing his livelihood on an automatic suspension. We ran a Licence Appeal establishing exceptional hardship — no public-transport route, dependent parent, no alternative employment in his catchment. The Magistrate overturned the suspension entirely.

"If I lost my licence I lost my job. They got me back on the road the same week."

— P.B., Courier
